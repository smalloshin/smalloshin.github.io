Thanks for helping me evaluate Frictionary, a system for automatically extracting and aggregating problems from help requests, bug reports, discussion boards, and any other textual source of software problem discussions.

The idea behind Frictionary is simple:

  1. Gather hundreds of thousands of help requests, bug reports, and other types of discussion board posts about Firefox. This prototype is based of of ~103,000 posts between November 2009 and July 2010, from support.mozilla.org, superuser.com, Firefox forums on mozillaZine, and Mozilla bug reports.
  2. Use natural language processing and information extraction techniques to identify problem topics in each post such as "Firefox crashing" and "bookmark deleting", and the many other examples in the screenshot above.
  3. Aggregate these topics and present them over time to see trends in users' experiences with Firefox.

Because this is a research prototype, I have a few disclaimers:

  • The user interface currently eats a lot of RAM to keep much of the data in memory. I apologize if this degrades the experience or compels you to close your favorite tabs :)
  • The data is this demo is already more than a year old. When thinking about it's utility, imagine back to the Firefox 3.6 and 4.0 releases in 2009 and 2010.
  • I've tested the demo in Chrome 12 and greater, Firefox 4 and greater, and Safari 5.0 and greater.
